Ultimate Frisbee

Introduction

Ultimate, or more commonly known as Ultimate Frisbee, is a non-contact team sport where players make use of a flying disc for the game. Ultimate is different from other sports in the sense that it is self-refereed (in most cases), and requires players to display the 'Spirit of the Game' aka sportsmanship. The playing field for a default game of Ultimate is a rectangle (64m by 37m), with endzones (18m deep) at the two ends of the rectangles. Each team usually consists of seven players on the line for each point.

Basic Rules

At the beginning of a point, both teams will line up on the front of their endzone. The team on defence will throw the disc over, and the offense will pick up the disc from where it lands and begin the game. To score, the team would need to complete a pass into the opponent’s endzone.

Some points to note:

  • Players can’t run with the disc (travel foul)
  • The person with the disc has ten seconds to throw the disc before the stall count is up.
  • When a pass is not completed (e.g. out of bounds, drop, block, interception), the possession of the disc is changed.

Developments

Ultimate has been gaining traction globally over the past decade, and has since been recognised as an official sport by the International Olympic Committee (IOC). There has also been much effort to push for Ultimate to be included as an offical sport in the 2028 Olympics Games!
